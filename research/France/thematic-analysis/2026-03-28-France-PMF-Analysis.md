• **Regulatory triangulation:** **Theme 3** (**pay transparency**, **CSE**, **offer localisation**) threads **French employment law** together with **GDPR** data-protection duties (purpose limitation, transparency, retention). **Themes 1–2** (**AI** matching, **omnichannel** / scheduling) triangulate **EU AI Act** high-risk recruitment AI with **GDPR Art. 22** (human review of significant automated decisions) and **Art. 35** (**DPIA** where high-risk profiling applies).

## Product Roadmap Impact Summary

**RICE model:** `RICE = (Reach × Impact × Confidence) / Effort` with **Impact** on **1.0–3.0** composite scale (strategy-weighted), **Confidence** as decimal. **Reach** = approximate **France-relevant enterprise recruiter seats** at risk in competitive / compliance narratives (**order-of-magnitude**). **Effort** = **person-months** (engineering + product + design, indicative).

**060 (Legal) preview flags (non-binding):** **Rec 1** (**EU AI Act-ready hiring AI packaging**): recruitment and selection AI is **high-risk** under **EU AI Act Annex III**; expect **DPIA-style** diligence, technical documentation, and logging expectations alongside deployer transparency; triangulate with **GDPR Art. 35** where processing is likely high-risk and **Art. 22** (no solely automated legal/significant-effect decisions without meaningful **human oversight**). **Rec 2** (**Paradox / conversational scheduling**): position activation so **human schedulers and recruiters remain in the loop**; avoid enablement that implies **fully unsupervised** automation of candidate interactions or decisions. **Cross-cutting:** **GDPR** transparency and lawful basis; **pay transparency** and **salary history** restrictions; **EU data residency** / transfer narratives; **060** full review at orchestrator Step 10 should refine wording.
