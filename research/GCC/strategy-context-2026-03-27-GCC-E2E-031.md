## Strategic priorities (Q2 2026)

### Priority 1: GCC market readiness (strategic)

- **Description**: GCC expansion is a 2026 strategic priority; product gaps are deal blockers. Work must close parity vs. regional suite expectations and local compliance.
- **Target outcomes**: 10 new GCC customer wins by end Q2 (baseline: 3); zero product-related deal blockers in the GCC pipeline; zero P0 bugs in GCC features.
- **Key initiatives**: Candidate communication (WhatsApp, SMS – cultural fit, high engagement); nationalisation compliance (KSA Nitaqat, UAE Emiratisation, Kuwait labour-law reporting); localisation (Arabic RTL, country-specific workflows); job boards (Bayt.com, GulfTalent via Broadbean – verify partnership coverage before recommending native build).
- **GCC relevance**: **Direct** – this is the quarter’s top Recruiting priority for the region.

### Priority 2: AI candidate matching (differentiation)

- **Description**: Realise HiredScore ROI; position AI as a differentiator with responsible use (bias detection, explainability, EU AI Act posture).
- **Target outcomes**: 5 beta tenants on AI matching by end Q2; ~20% reduction in time-to-fill for beta customers; AI matching service reliability (e.g. 95% uptime target in Q2 product metrics).
- **GCC relevance**: **High** – global initiative; GCC deals will still expect credible AI + compliance narrative (human oversight, transparency).

### Priority 3: Core ATS parity (table stakes)

- **Description**: Win competitive cycles on essential recruiter workflows, mobile, integrations, and scheduling automation.
- **Target outcomes**: Parity with SAP/Oracle on key workflows; eliminate “missing basic feature” sales objections.
- **Key initiatives**: Bulk candidate actions; mobile recruiter experience; background check integrations (HireRight, Checkr); interview scheduling friction reduction (Paradox activation).
- **GCC relevance**: **High** – table stakes for enterprise GCC buyers comparing suite vendors.

## Annual TA vision (March 2026 PDF) – themes for alignment

These themes sit **above** the quarterly priority list and should inform **130** narrative and **120** “why now” framing:

1. **One seamless, purpose-built TA suite** – Recruiting & Candidate Engagement as foundation; **Recruiting & Mobility Agents** (HiredScore) for matching, screening, coaching; **Candidate Experience Agent** (Paradox) for conversational engagement and scheduling – with explicit **human-in-the-loop** positioning for agentic workflows.
2. **Thoughtful AI** – Market narrative: high AI adoption in HR/TA but material time lost to rework; success = **redesigning work**, not maximising raw AI surface area.
3. **Key TA themes for 2026 and beyond** (deck): **Maximise talent potential**; **Real process automation**; **Standardise excellence**; **World-class user experience**; plus direction toward a **consumer-grade interface** for all users.
4. **Near-term product evidence** (PDF) aligned to GCC E2E research:
   - **WhatsApp messaging** (EA January 2026) – in-platform recruiter–candidate comms; collaborative panel pattern; personalisation, multi-language; fits **Priority 1** comms track.
   - **Enhanced candidate communication consent (EA)** – pairs with WhatsApp and regulatory expectations in GCC/EU contexts.
   - **Interview scheduling automation** and **advanced interview coordination** – multi-channel invites/reminders including **email, SMS, or WhatsApp**; uses Interview Team Optimisation – aligns **Priority 3** and agent roadmap.
   - **Improved recruiter UX for screening & selection** – unified HiredScore + Workday experience (dynamic list, carousel) – aligns **Priority 2/3**.

**Caution**: PDF includes forward-looking Paradox/HiredScore roadmaps marked confidential; treat timing and naming as **planning-level**, not committed GA promises, consistent with the deck’s safe-harbour slide.

## Competitive positioning

### Workday differentiation (Q2 doc + PDF narrative)

1. **Suite depth** – HCM + Recruiting + Talent + Learning vs. point solutions  
2. **AI-powered platform** – HiredScore (matching, agents, explainability trajectory) + Paradox (scheduling, candidate experience agent) vs. manual workflows  
3. **Compliance-first** – GDPR, EU AI Act readiness, global privacy – enterprise posture  
4. **Enterprise scale** – Fortune 500 workflows, security model, global rollouts  
5. **Orchestrated TA** – Hiring Navigator / agents as “next best action” in recruiter and manager workflows (PDF)

### Competitive vulnerabilities (Q2 doc)

- **Job board coverage** – Broadbean partnership gaps in niche regions (validate Broadbean before proposing net-new native board builds)  
- **Mobile** – SAP strong on mobile recruiter experience; Workday catching up  
- **Interview scheduling** – Paradox capability often **activation-dependent**, not automatic win in bake-offs

### Key messaging (for **101** / **130** competitive slides)

- GCC: **Local comms + nationalisation + Arabic** are **buyer must-haves**; position Workday’s **roadmap and activation** story honestly vs. SAP/Oracle **native regional** claims.  
- Global: **Single suite + responsible AI + human-in-the-loop agents** vs. fragmented tools.

## RICE business impact guidance (this mission)

Use alongside customer evidence for **Composite impact** = (Business impact + Customer impact) / 2.

| Business impact   | Score | Criteria (GCC-E2E-031) |
|-------------------|-------|-------------------------|
| Strategic priority | **3.0** | Directly advances **Q2 Priority 1 (GCC readiness)** or **KR1** (10 GCC wins): e.g. WhatsApp/SMS in core UX, nationalisation reporting, Arabic/RTL blockers, GCC deal-breaker parity |
| Strong alignment   | **2.0** | **Priority 2–3** or annual themes: AI matching activation, explainability, core ATS parity, Paradox scheduling, bulk actions, mobile – clearly supporting GCC enterprise sales even if not uniquely “GCC” |
| Neutral            | **1.0** | Useful quality-of-life; no strong Q2 OKR linkage |
| Weak alignment     | **0.5** | Low strategic urgency or crowded later roadmap |
| Misaligned         | **0.25** | Conflicts with **What’s NOT a priority** or distracts from OKRs without exceptional evidence |

**Examples** (illustrative):

- WhatsApp + consent + recruiter workflow in GCC: **3.0** business impact (Priority 1 + PDF EA alignment).  
- Nationalisation / Emiratisation / Nitaqat native reporting: **3.0**.  
- Interview scheduling depth (Paradox activation, coordinator UX): **2.0** (Priority 3 + PDF).  
- Career site redesign for GCC only: **0.5** (explicitly not Q2).
