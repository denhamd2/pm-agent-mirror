## Key Findings per Participant

### P1 – Lead, Cyber Security and Campus Hiring, Accenture

**Role context:** Leads cyber security and campus hiring across Egypt, GCC and Africa; team of ~5; uses Workday for performance, events, and end-to-end reqs.

**Key quotes:**

> "WhatsApp is an absolute necessary … that's how I reach out to my candidates for quick closures … if workday can have integration with WhatsApp … it's just going to be absolutely brilliant."

> "I'm liable to hit … 20% Emiratisation, 60% Saudization, 50% Kuwaitisation on my hiring … it's quite important that we track those metrics on workday."

> "If I can click my fingers and have it, I'd rather have that [scheduling]."

> "The dashboards … very couldn't read them … it gave me a headache … I'll need to sort of download it and create my own dashboards."

**Pain points:**

- Moving candidates across reqs blocked unless tagged to every req; admin tax for leads  
- Notes before formal screen stage feel blocked  
- Historic funnel metrics per req unclear in-product  
- Offer configuration rigid; long turnaround for new grades  
- Interview scheduling not end-to-end in Workday; external tool in use  
- KSA-style interview rules (notice period, panel composition) need system support  

**JTBD (from `docs/jtbd-recruiting-hr-professional-and-manager.md`):** When hiring across regulated GCC markets, recruiters need to prove quota compliance and move candidates quickly, so they can hit targets without offline spreadsheets or policy risk.

### P2 – Performance and Innovation Manager, Talent Acquisition, Baker Hughes

**Role context:** Functional owner for Workday Recruiting; partners with technical Workday team; global scope with explicit Middle East / GCC considerations.

**Key quotes:**

> "Can it be more seamless … rather than having to go through multiple different tabs … when they're trying to go through 100 candidates or 200 candidates."

> "I don't think the boolean search is that strong on workday … I would definitely want to see a much more improved boolean search capabilities."

> "We have two million candidates in our workday database … can I see who else is matching that requirement."

> "We tried to utilize the workday scheduling capability … it felt more complicated than scheduling a meeting via Outlook."

> "Having something like a WhatsApp … would be helpful especially in markets like GCC and Saudi."

> "Almost say 40% or more actually coming via a mobile or a handheld device."

**Pain points:**

- Candidate grid navigation across tabs at high volume  
- Search and discovery across large talent database  
- Scheduling UX vs Outlook  
- Nationalisation tracked via custom fields; penalties for missing targets  
- Career site branding limits; apply redirect friction  
- Campaign channels beyond email (WhatsApp, etc.)  

**JTBD:** When operating a global enterprise ATS, TA operations need one ecosystem with strong recruiter throughput and local channel coverage, so they can reduce tool sprawl and protect compliance.

---

### P3 – Product Owner, Talent and Resourcing, Shell

**Role context:** Owns talent and resourcing landscape with heavy Workday use since 2020; interfaces with recruiters, operations, and HR digital; GCC sits in franchise / JV cluster with lower volume.

**Key quotes:**

> "Difficulties and challenges with workday is its reporting functionality … we did have to resort building a dashboard separately … in PowerBI."

> "For offers specifically the Arabic countries … work day docs … it would just be squares rather than the actual characters."

> "We try to also keep it official … we just avoid the company name being compromised … WhatsApp … classified as something that we just we can't use for official business."

> "Struggle is really being able to know … across all of the hundreds of CV that we get knowing immediately who we should be paying attention to."

**Pain points:**

- Native dashboards insufficient for leadership and operational cuts  
- Arabic rendering in offer documentation  
- Franchise markets: local compliance reporting sometimes manual (e.g. Excel)  
- High application volume vs few roles creates prioritisation problem  
- Enterprise policy restricts WhatsApp despite market norms elsewhere  

**JTBD:** When governing a global programme, HR digital needs trustworthy reporting and compliant comms channels, so low-volume markets still meet the law without unsanctioned tools.

## Synthesized Themes (for 120 PMF integration)

1. **Nationalisation and workforce compliance** – OOTB tracking and reporting vs custom fields; penalties and quotas (KSA, UAE, Kuwait).  
2. **Interview scheduling and statutory guardrails** – Outlook/Teams friction; KSA notice and panel rules; need auditable workflows.  
3. **Recruiter efficiency: grid, search, AI** – Tab density, boolean limits, database-wide matching (HiredScore interest).  
4. **Omnichannel engagement** – WhatsApp/SMS demand vs enterprise policy; email-only campaigns insufficient for some segments.  
5. **Reporting and dashboard abandonment** – PowerBI/Excel exits; unreadable or inflexible in-product views.  
6. **Offers, documents, and localization** – Offer rigidity; structured uploads; Arabic/RTL in documents; mobile apply.

**Triangulation note (this study):** Themes are grounded in **customer** interviews; **CSV** Win/Loss file (`filtered_gcc_opps.csv`) echoes **scheduling/integration** and **job posting / competitor** pressure (see **120** report).
