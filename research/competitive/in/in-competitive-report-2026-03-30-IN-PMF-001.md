## Executive Summary

India talent acquisition technology applies pressure to Workday in three patterns. First, **India-native HR suites** (Darwinbox, Keka, greytHR) combine recruiting, payroll, and local board workflows with background verification partners, chiefly SpringVerify, plus growing Aadhaar/PAN automation narratives in ATS stories (Zoho Recruit and marketplace extensions are the clearest public example). Second, **global enterprise stacks** (SAP SuccessFactors with SmartRecruiters, Oracle Fusion with Taleo legacy, iCIMS as best-of-breed ATS) set expectations for AI and deep integrations, amplified by March 2026 SAP and Oracle announcements. Third, **job board infrastructure** (Naukri dominant; Shine, TimesJobs; Monster / foundit in flux) shapes multipost language in RFPs.

For new market entry, Workday’s credible anchors remain enterprise governance: **UDMF**, native background check **business process** framework, configurable privacy levers for DPDP-aligned programmes (legal interpretation via **060**), and global hire-to-pay cohesion where Payroll is in scope. Honest gaps include native direct Naukri-class multipost (Broadbean-class workaround per **010**), native WhatsApp in core Recruiting UI, advanced semantic AI without entitled SKUs, and native UIDAI Aadhaar eKYC (not productised; vendor or custom). A fresh Deployment Agent thread (`d7ae197d-1a31-43fb-a44d-6831de3b71b3`, 30 March 2026) **conflicted** with prior **DA-IN004** / **DA-IN-PMF-002** on native SMS to +91. Until Professional Services or authoritative documentation reconciles this, retain the **True Gap** classification for battle cards and avoid widening native SMS claims.

## Market Landscape Overview

### Segment A — India HRMS / ATS bundles

Darwinbox signals scale with March 2025 $140M funding (Partners Group, KKR; newsroom and trade press) and AI-led high-volume screening content. SpringVerify integration supports Know Your Candidate positioning with API automation and WhatsApp-friendly candidate steps (partner blog). Keka targets mid-market with multipost breadth and SpringVerify on the marketplace; developer documentation describes API-first background verification. greytHR anchors payroll trust and adds Recruit with Naukri, Hirist, and IIM Jobs via Zwayam Amplify (Freshdesk/help articles); February 2026 release notes reference Naukri Gulf multipost, sharpening the local board story relative to Workday’s multiposter architecture.

### Segment B — Global enterprise TA

SAP’s March 2026 SmartRecruiters for SuccessFactors narrative matters for India shared services and MNC templates: unified navigation, synced org data, and paired AI assistants (Joule and Winston AI) per SAP News and HR Brew. Oracle pairs Fusion Agentic headlines with Recruiting Booster and Career Coach; Taleo remains a migration conversation (oracle.com/taleo). iCIMS brings Prime Connector background screening (for example First Advantage) and a strong global ATS brand in selected deals (vendor newsroom).

### Segment C — Job boards and verification services

Naukri remains the default India board for employer reach; multipost typically requires paid board modules and aggregator or ATS-native integrations (for example Greenhouse partner documentation). Shine advertises ATS integration for employers (recruiter.shine.com). SpringVerify, AuthBridge, and First Advantage compete on API depth, India coverage claims, and ATS embedding, often overlapping Workday’s partner ecosystem.

### Regulatory backdrop

Third-party legal summaries describe phased DPDP obligations into 2027 on some readings (for example TCSA, Glocert, Mondaq). Position Workday as enabling customer programmes (consent, retention, rights workflows) rather than implying statutory compliance out of the box. Route binding interpretation to counsel and **060**.

## Feature Gap Analysis (Candidate Verification, Fraud, High Volume)

### Candidate verification and fraud prevention

Competitors package background verification with in-app status, WhatsApp steps, and automatic document pull (Zoho SpringVerify blog, August 2025). Workday matches on audit-grade process design and vendor flexibility but must not claim native UIDAI verification. UDMF is a clear differentiator where mid-market suite dedupe depth is not evidenced publicly.

### High-volume hiring

Marketing claims on applications per role and AI pre-screens set demo bars (greytHR LinkedIn soft-launch statistics; Darwinbox AI screening blogs). Workday should anchor on operational controls (bulk actions, process rigour) and entitled AI (HiredScore, Workday AI) with governance (human review, logging) for EU AI Act and India risk committees.

### Channels and boards

WhatsApp and +91 SMS are cultural defaults. Keep battle card language conservative on native +91 SMS until the Deployment Agent conflict is reconciled. Naukri multipost needs validated Broadbean coverage per deal and clear architecture diagrams for IT security reviews.

## Competitive Positioning Recommendations

• Lead enterprise trust: UDMF, security operating model, global template, single candidate record across HCM.  
• Co-sell India BGV leaders customers already choose (SpringVerify, AuthBridge, First Advantage) via certified integration patterns.  
• Package DPDP collateral with **060**: how consent, retention, and erasure map to Workday configuration (no legal guarantee).  
• Respond to SAP SmartRecruiters with integration complexity and TCO framing where single-suite Workday is in scope; avoid dismissive tone where buyers want best-of-breed ATS depth.  
• Acknowledge Zoho and greytHR speed to value; win on scale, governance, and extensibility for larger enterprises and complex security.

## Battle Cards (Quick Reference — Top Five)

### 1. Darwinbox

• **Strengths:** India brand, AI volume story, SpringVerify flow, funded international growth.  
• **Weaknesses:** Enterprise global template and security scrutiny versus US/EU peers (deal-specific); hire-to-pay depth varies versus full Workday footprint.  
• **Workday talk track:** UDMF, global HCM, BGV framework plus partner choice; SKU clarity on AI.  
• **Proof to request:** Segregation of duties, audit logs, cross-border data flow architecture.

### 2. SAP (SuccessFactors + SmartRecruiters)

• **Strengths:** Incumbent ERP, March 2026 integrated TA story, dual AI assistants.  
• **Weaknesses:** Dual product complexity and migration uncertainty for some customers.  
• **Workday talk track:** Unified cloud architecture, candidate experience, skills intelligence on one platform where true.  
• **Proof to request:** Integration roadmap, data latency, licence bundle mechanics.

### 3. Oracle (Fusion + Taleo)

• **Strengths:** Agentic 2026 narrative, Recruiting Booster, legacy Taleo base.  
• **Weaknesses:** Perceived upgrade load from Taleo; AI governance questions at regulated employers.  
• **Workday talk track:** Human oversight, configurable privacy, honest mobile strategy.  
• **Proof to request:** Agent audit trail, data residency choices, candidate UX benchmarks.

### 4. Zoho Recruit

• **Strengths:** TCO, ecosystem, Aadhaar/PAN automation narratives, marketplace density.  
• **Weaknesses:** Enterprise security and process depth for large regulated employers.  
• **Workday talk track:** Enterprise referenceability, UDMF, integration governance with IT.  
• **Proof to request:** SOC reports, role design at scale, complex approval chains.

### 5. iCIMS

• **Strengths:** ATS depth, BGV Prime Connectors, talent marketing breadth where licensed.  
• **Weaknesses:** Separate from core HCM unless integrated; India payroll not core to iCIMS.  
• **Workday talk track:** Single HCM record, India payroll when in scope, reduced integration tax.  
• **Proof to request:** Total cost of integrations versus suite.

## Strategic Synthesis (Six Hats — Condensed)

• **White:** Funding, product launches, and board integrations are documented in public sources listed in the scan.  
• **Red:** Buyers feel urgency on fraud prevention and speed; emotional pull toward familiar India vendors.  
• **Black:** Underestimating Zoho or greytHR TCO, or overclaiming SMS or Aadhaar, damages trust.  
• **Yellow:** Partner ecosystem overlap (SpringVerify) lets Workday show parity on BGV outcomes when implemented well.  
• **Green:** A “Know Your Candidate” playbook pairing UDMF, BGV, and privacy automation is differentiated if messaged clearly.  
• **Blue:** Next step is PS reconciliation on +91 SMS and quarterly refresh of board lists (Monster / foundit).
