## Strategic priorities (Q2 2026)

### Priority 1: GCC market readiness (strategic)

- **Description**: GCC expansion is the headline 2026 priority; product gaps are treated as sales blockers. Scope includes candidate communication (WhatsApp, SMS), nationalisation (KSA Nitaqat, UAE Emiratisation, Kuwait reporting), Arabic RTL and country workflows, and job boards (Bayt.com, GulfTalent via Broadbean).
- **Target outcomes**: 10 GCC customer wins by end Q2; zero product-related deal blockers in GCC pipeline.
- **India relevance**: **Indirect**. Sets the quality bar for regional readiness (compliance, messaging, local distribution). **101** should still map India competitors to global parity, but **RICE Business Impact** for purely GCC-only scope stays low when the pipeline mission is India unless evidence shows generalised enterprise need.

### Priority 2: AI candidate matching (differentiation)

- **Description**: Activate HiredScore for high-volume customers; AI ranking, shortlisting, predictive time-to-fill; bias detection and explainability framed for EU AI Act alignment.
- **Target outcomes**: 5 beta tenants on AI matching by end Q2; ~20% time-to-fill reduction for beta customers.
- **India relevance**: **Direct**. India scale hiring and IT/services-heavy accounts align with AI matching ROI; **120** should score AI-heavy recommendations as **strong strategic alignment** when evidence supports adoption, with India **DPDP** and consent called out in Step 2 PESTEL/Legal (not assumed here).

### Priority 3: Core ATS parity (table stakes)

- **Description**: Bulk candidate actions, mobile recruiter experience, background check integrations (HireRight, Checkr), interview scheduling friction reduction via Paradox activation.
- **Target outcomes**: Parity with SAP/Oracle on key workflows; no missing basic feature sales objections.
- **India relevance**: **Direct**. India growth depends on table-stakes wins in high-volume workflows; **101** should prioritise gaps that surface in enterprise RFPs and recruiter efficiency (bulk, mobile, scheduling, integrations).

## Competitive positioning

**Workday differentiation** (Q2 themes):

1. **Suite depth**: HCM + Recruiting + Talent + Learning versus point ATS vendors.  
2. **AI-powered**: HiredScore + Paradox when activated versus manual workflows.  
3. **Compliance-first**: GDPR, EU AI Act, global privacy positioning versus reactive competitors.  
4. **Enterprise scale**: Fortune 500 workflows, security model, global rollouts.

**Competitive vulnerabilities** (watch for India-specific evidence in **101** / **105**):

- Job board coverage gaps (Broadbean partnership limits in niche or local channels).  
- Mobile recruiter experience versus SAP.  
- Interview scheduling parity when Paradox is not activated.

**Key messaging** (GTM / **130** Product Strategy hooks):

- Land **trust and compliance** (DPDP, consent, auditability) with **volume efficiency** (AI matching, bulk actions, scheduling).  
- Do not promise **native** job board builds where **Broadbean** expansion is the stated strategy (see **010-style-guide** / product context).
