## Strategic Priorities (Q2 2026)

**Priority 1: GCC market readiness (strategic)**

- **Description**: Close product and localisation gaps blocking GCC sales; align candidate communication and compliance reporting with KSA, UAE, and Kuwait expectations.
- **Target outcomes**: 10 GCC customer wins by end Q2; zero product-related deal blockers in GCC pipeline; Arabic RTL and nationalisation-style reporting themes.
- **India relevance**: **Indirect.** Corporate OKRs and Q2 headline investment centre on GCC. India benefits from parallel themes (messaging channels, compliance-first narrative) but is **not** Q2 Priority 1 in the quarterly doc. For **this** mission, treat India as **strategic for research and roadmap narrative**, not as the corporate Q2 OKR headline.

**Priority 2: AI candidate matching (differentiation)**

- **Description**: Prove HiredScore ROI through activation, ranking, shortlisting, and predictive insights; bias detection and explainability framed for regulated markets (including EU AI Act narrative in quarterly doc).
- **Target outcomes**: 5 beta tenants on AI matching by end Q2; ~20% time-to-fill improvement for beta customers.
- **India relevance**: **Strong.** Quarterly doc explicitly ties HiredScore to **high-volume** customers. India’s **high-volume recruiting** scenarios (bulk reqs, large applicant funnels, MSP/GIC hiring) are a **primary differentiator** for how Workday should win against local and global ATS players. Paradox scheduling and multi-channel outreach (email, SMS, WhatsApp in roadmap) support throughput and candidate response in high-volume markets.

**Priority 3: Core ATS parity (table stakes)**

- **Description**: Bulk candidate actions, mobile recruiter experience, background check vendor integrations (HireRight, Checkr), reduced scheduling friction (Paradox activation).
- **Target outcomes**: Parity with SAP/Oracle on key workflows; fewer “missing basic feature” sales objections.
- **India relevance**: **Strong for KYC / BGV.** Background check integrations and **trust/signal** features (PDF: social login, fraudulent application detection phase 1, purge plans) map directly to **Know Your Candidate**, **identity verification**, and **BGV workflow** expectations. India-specific nuance (Aadhaar, government ID, vendor ecosystem) sits **at the intersection of parity and compliance**, even when not spelled out in the global priority list.

### India strategic assessment (INDIA-PMF-005)

- **Why India matters now**: Quarterly strategy lists India under **scale growth** with concrete **customer win target** and **DPDP** plus **job boards**. Mission driver is **new market entry**, so research should stress **first-wave positioning**: compliance-ready hiring, high-volume efficiency, and **trust** in candidate identity and BGV.
- **High-volume recruiting**: Align with **HiredScore activation**, **improved screening/selection UX** (PDF “In Design”), **dynamic candidate lists**, and **automation** (scheduling, re-engagement). This is the **sharp edge** for India PMF: throughput, recruiter capacity, and quality at scale.
- **KYC / identity / Aadhaar / BGV**: Align with **core ATS parity** (BGV integrations), **fraudulent application detection (phase 1)**, **social and LinkedIn sign-in** (next-wave roadmap), **purge and consent** enhancements, and **compliance-first** brand. Product strategy should **not** assume Aadhaar is a named Q2 corporate deliverable; treat it as **India market requirement space** to validate via CI, 105, and legal/regulatory PESTEL.
- **Constraints**: **Broadbean** is the path for **local job boards** (011 product context); recommend **partnership coverage checks**, not native job board builds. **Career site redesign** is explicitly **not** Q2 priority, which may **tension** with candidate-experience expectations in competitive India markets.

## Competitive Positioning

**Workday differentiation (Q2 themes, India lens)**

1. **Suite depth**: HCM + Recruiting + Talent + Learning for enterprise rollouts and global capability centres.  
2. **AI-powered**: HiredScore matching and grading explainability; Paradox for engagement and scheduling; Hiring Navigator style orchestration over time.  
3. **Compliance-first**: DPDP called out for India in regional table; global narrative on privacy and AI governance supports **trust** positioning for sensitive identity and BGV data.  
4. **Enterprise scale**: Fortune 500 patterns, security model, high-volume workflows when HiredScore and automation are activated.

**Competitive vulnerabilities (watch for India)**

- **Job board coverage**: Gaps vs local boards if Broadbean coverage is thin; verify before claiming parity.  
- **Mobile recruiter experience**: Quarterly doc notes SAP mobile strength; relevant for field and high-volume recruiter personas in India.  
- **Scheduling**: Paradox value realisation depends on **activation**, not shelfware.  
- **Regional depth**: GCC is Q1 investment; prospects may compare **India localisation** (DPDP, ID, BGV) to vendors with longer India-specific roadmaps.

**Key messaging (GTM / deck hooks)**

- “Compliant, high-trust hiring at scale: data protection, auditable processes, and integrations that support verification and screening.”  
- “AI that augments recruiters in **high-volume** funnels: explainable matching, faster shortlisting, and orchestrated engagement across channels.”
