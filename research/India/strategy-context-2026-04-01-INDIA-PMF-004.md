## Strategic Priorities (Q2 2026)

**Priority 1: GCC market readiness (strategic)**  
• **Description**: Remove GCC deal blockers through candidate communication (WhatsApp, SMS), nationalisation compliance, Arabic RTL, and Broadbean job boards (e.g. Bayt, GulfTalent).  
• **Target outcomes**: 10 GCC customer wins; zero product-related GCC pipeline blockers.  
• **India relevance**: **Indirect.** Sets the bar for **regional compliance + local channels**; India work should use the same pattern (local boards via Broadbean, privacy law rigour) without assuming GCC is the India backlog.

**Priority 2: AI candidate matching (differentiation)**  
• **Description**: Activate HiredScore for high-volume customers; ranking, shortlisting, predictive time-to-fill; bias detection and explainability (EU AI Act framing as global standard).  
• **Target outcomes**: 5 beta tenants on AI matching by end Q2; ~20% time-to-fill improvement for beta cohort.  
• **India relevance**: **High** for **high volume** India TA; positions **Know Your Candidate** as input quality and governance for AI (explainability, consent, fair processing under DPDP).

**Priority 3: Core ATS parity (table stakes)**  
• **Description**: Bulk candidate actions, mobile recruiter improvements, BGC vendor integrations (HireRight, Checkr), Paradox activation for scheduling friction.  
• **Target outcomes**: Parity with SAP/Oracle on key workflows; fewer "missing basic feature" objections.  
• **India relevance**: **High** for throughput and operational scale; BGC integrations and bulk actions support **high volume** and **verification** journeys.

**India strategic assessment**

• **Market entry lens (mission driver)**: Positioning and backlog should assume **competitive, compliance-aware** buyers; **Know Your Candidate** and **high volume** are credible wedges if tied to measurable pipeline and activation outcomes.

## Competitive Positioning

**Workday differentiation**

1. **Suite depth**: HCM + Recruiting + Talent + Learning versus point ATS  
2. **AI-powered**: HiredScore + Paradox versus manual workflows  
3. **Compliance-first**: GDPR, EU AI Act, global privacy posture  
4. **Enterprise scale**: Fortune 500 patterns, security, global rollout  

**Competitive vulnerabilities**

• Job board coverage gaps in niche regions (verify **Broadbean** before proposing native boards)  
• Mobile recruiter experience versus SAP  
• Interview scheduling requires **Paradox activation** (not on by default)  

**Key messaging (sales / GTM)**

• Lead with **trusted, governed AI** and **enterprise scale**, not feature checklists alone.  
• For India: pair **DPDP-aligned** handling of candidate data with **high-volume efficiency** (bulk, matching activation, scheduling).  
• **Know Your Candidate**: frame as **faster, fairer decisions** with **minimised data** and **auditable** processes, not as surveillance.

## What's NOT a Priority (Q2)

Explicitly de-prioritised in the quarterly doc:

• Career site redesign (deferred to Q3)  
• Talent pool AI recommendations (deferred to H2)  
• Video interview integrations beyond HireVue (low demand)

**Analyst note**: Recommendations that mainly beautify career sites or expand marketing automation should score **low Business Impact** for Q2 unless tied to a documented India deal blocker.
