## Political

**Labour code consolidation and timing**  
India’s four consolidated labour codes (including the **Industrial Relations Code**) were widely reported as **notified** (e.g. November 2025 reporting) with **implementation** expectations discussed for **2026** (including **April 2026** commentary in trade press). The **Industrial Relations Code** introduces **fixed-term employment** across sectors with **parity** of wages and benefits with permanent workers for the contract period, and specific rules on non-renewal versus retrenchment (Lexology / PRS / MoL summaries). For **high-volume hiring**, this increases the need for **contract tracking**, **tenure visibility**, and **compliant offer and separation** workflows in the same platforms that manage sourcing and onboarding.

**Policy narrative: jobs, skills, and formalisation**  
Political emphasis on **formal job creation**, **GCC** expansion, and **digital public infrastructure** continues to shape employer expectations that hiring stacks support **scale**, **auditability**, and **integration** with statutory and benefits-related processes (ESI/PF narratives appear frequently in HR tech vendor positioning; labour ministry clarifications on **Aadhaar** use in benefits contexts illustrate sensitivity of **digital ID** in employment).

**Regulatory institutions**  
The **Data Protection Board of India** and **MeitY**-led implementation of **DPDP** move privacy from “drafting” to **staged enforcement** (see Legal), which is **political** as well as legal because penalties and sectoral overlays (RBI, SEBI, IRDAI) can diverge.

**Product implication**  
Workday Recruiting should align **high-volume and contingent** hiring scenarios with **contract type**, **fixed-term**, and **parity** reporting needs as codes roll out, while providing **audit trails** and handoffs to **HCM/Payroll** so Indian employers can defend workforce decisions; politically sensitive **ID** steps must stay **consent-led** and **role-specific**, not bundled into generic apply flows.

## Economic

**HR technology and ATS market growth**  
Industry reporting sizes **India HR technology** in the **hundreds of millions USD** with **mid-single-digit to high-single-digit CAGR** through early 2030s (e.g. MarketsandMarkets-style **USD ~806M** base year and **~USD 1.45B** by ~2030 narrative; IMARC **India ATS** **USD 0.30B** in **2024** toward **USD 0.50B** by **2033** at **~7.2% CAGR**). Drivers cited include **AI**, **cloud**, **mobile**, and **compliance automation**.

**High-volume hiring cycles**  
India **IT services** campus and fresher hiring is a structural **volume** driver: trade reporting points to **~150,000 fresher** hiring intentions for **FY26** and large firm-level targets (**TCS**, **Infosys**, **Wipro**, **Cognizant**), with **GCCs** absorbing a significant share of graduate demand. **Naukri JobSpeak** indices in **2025** showed **strong white-collar growth** with **non-IT** and **fresher** momentum called out by vendor commentary.

**GCC / capability centre expansion**  
Analyst and industry blogs cite **1,700+ GCCs**, **~2M professionals**, and **double-digit** revenue growth narratives for **2025–2026**, intensifying competition for **digital and AI skills** and raising the bar for **time-to-hire**, **governance**, and **cross-border** people data operations.

**Macro labour market**  
Press summaries of **PLFS**-style statistics (via ET / HR press) indicate **educated unemployment** improvement but persistent **youth** challenges; **high-volume** programmes must handle **sheer applicant counts** without sacrificing **compliance** or **candidate rights**.

## Social

**Mobile-first candidates and recruiters**  
India’s **mobile data** consumption and **5G** uptake (e.g. **Nokia MBiT**-style reporting of **~31 GB/month** average usage in **2025**, **5G** share of traffic) reinforce that **apply**, **messaging**, and **interview** journeys are **mobile-primary** for many cohorts; recruiter tooling must not assume **desktop-only** operations for **high-volume** funnels.

**Trust, fraud, and credential risk**  
High-profile **recruitment scams** and **credential fraud** cases (e.g. **teacher recruitment** credential investigations; **fake DRDO** hiring rings; **Pune IT** candidate fee fraud reported in **Moneycontrol**) elevate **employer** demand for **Know Your Candidate** discipline, **document provenance**, and **BGV** orchestration with **candidate-visible** fairness.

**Language and diversity**  
English remains dominant in **white-collar** TA tech, but **Hindi** and regional language expectations appear in **employer** RFPs; **diversity** and **inclusion** narratives intersect with **AI screening** concerns (see Technological / Legal).

## Technological

**AI in hiring and governance**  
India’s **MeitY**-published **India AI Governance Guidelines** (reported **November 2025**) and **RBI** **FREE-AI** framework (**August 2025** for financial services) signal **principle-based** oversight moving toward **institutional** structures (governance groups, safety institute narratives). Parliamentary **bills** on **AI ethics** and **employee rights** (e.g. **Artificial Intelligence (Ethics & Accountability) Bill, 2025**; **Protection of Rights of Employees** proposals) are **not yet settled law** but shape **enterprise** risk reviews for **resume screening** and **automated ranking**.

**HR cloud and automation**  
Vendor and systems integrator narratives (e.g. **Oracle** press with **Wipro** HR modernisation on cloud, **ZingHR** / industry blogs) stress **cloud HR**, **analytics**, and **automation**; **AI** features are marketed heavily in **ATS** and **HRMS** segments referenced in IMARC summaries.

**Identity stack**  
**UIDAI** materials and **2025** amendment commentary on **Aadhaar Authentication and Offline Verification** describe **consent-driven** and **app-based** verification evolution (e.g. **Aadhaar Verifiable Credential** concepts in legal summaries); this matters for **Know Your Candidate** product design even when Workday does **not** natively host **UIDAI** pipes.

## Environmental

**BRSR and workforce-related ESG disclosures**  
SEBI’s **BRSR** framework drives listed Indian companies toward **quantified** social metrics (training, gender mix, turnover, stability). **BRSR Core** **reasonable assurance** timelines extend through **FY 2026–27** for expanding cohorts of top listed entities per assurance guides and consultancy summaries.

**Operational footprint**  
Recruiting software’s **direct** environmental impact is small; the material **Environmental** angle for India enterprises is **reporting** and **employer brand** alignment when customers publish **workforce** sustainability metrics sourced from HR systems.

**DATA GAP**  
India-specific **per-hire carbon** or **green recruiting** metrics are **not** standardised in authoritative government datasets retrieved in this pass; treat **green job** reporting as **customer-specific** unless statutory forms emerge.

**Sources**

**Product implication**  
Workday should ensure **reporting** and **analytics** exports support **workforce composition**, **training hours**, and **turnover** fields that feed **BRSR** workflows, while staying neutral on **environmental** claims unless customers define methodologies; for IN-PMF-002 this is secondary to **volume** and **KYC** but relevant for **listed** India HQ accounts.

## Legal

**Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 and Rules 2025 (phased)**  
Authoritative legal summaries state **MeitY** notified the **Digital Personal Data Protection Rules, 2025** on **13 November 2025**, with **phased** commencement: immediate effect for **Board** establishment and certain provisions; **13 November 2026** for **consent manager** registration (**Rule 4**); **13 May 2027** for core obligations including **notice**, **consent**, **security**, **retention**, **rights**, and **cross-border** transfer rules (**Rules 3, 5–16, 22–23** per Hogan Lovells / JDSupra summary).  
**Cross-border** transfers follow a **permissive default** with power for the **Central Government** to **restrict** jurisdictions or categories (negative-list style commentary in Mondaq / K&S style notes); **Significant Data Fiduciary** rules may trigger **DPO in India**, **audits**, **DPIAs**, and **algorithmic** diligence.

**Breaches and accountability**  
Summaries require **without delay** notification to the **Board** and **72-hour** follow-up reporting, plus **data principal** notification without a fixed statutory delay for individuals in some summaries; penalties up to **₹250 crore** appear in commentary for certain violations (verify against final gazette text for deal documentation).

**Background verification under DPDP**  
BGV involves **high-risk** processing chains (identity, criminal, education, employment). Third-party summaries and BGV vendor legal content emphasise **explicit**, **purpose-specific**, **unbundled** **consent**; **minimisation**; **purpose limitation**; **retention and deletion**; and **fiduciary** liability for **processor** failures. Employers must maintain **contracts**, **audit**, and **evidence** of **deletion** (SpringVerify blog; HireRight compliance spotlight; employer checklists).

**Aadhaar**  
**2025** amendments and **UIDAI** guidance emphasise **structured**, **consent-driven** authentication and **offline** verification; **employers** must respect **proportionality** and **lawful basis** beyond “convenience”. Labour ministry clarifications on **ESI** contexts illustrate **Aadhaar** sensitivity in **employment** adjacent processes (Mondaq article chain).

**BFSI hiring**  
**RBI** **KYC** amendment directions (**2025**) tighten **CKYCR** responsibility and **customer** due diligence timelines; where banks hire at scale, **identity** and **screening** processes intersect with **regulatory** expectations, though **employment** BGV remains **employer-led** under **DPDP**.

**Product implication (Legal factor, extended)**  
Workday Recruiting must implement **granular** candidate **notices** and **consent** for **each** BGV data type, **withdrawal** handling, **retention schedules** and **deletion** evidence for **BGV artefacts**, **sub-processor** transparency, and **breach** playbooks that fit **Board** timing; **cross-border** SaaS architecture requires **customer** legal ownership of **transfer** risk after **May 2027** full operationalisation of transfer rules, with **India DPO** and **SDF** assessments handled at **customer** level unless Workday entity classification dictates otherwise; **Aadhaar** flows should be **partner-mediated** with **UIDAI** registration and **consent** patterns owned by the customer, avoiding **over-collection** that violates **minimisation** (e.g. **PAN**-sufficient paths where appropriate).

## Cross-factor synthesis (IN-PMF-002)

• **High-volume hiring** sits at the intersection of **Economic** scale (**IT**, **GCC**, **JobSpeak** growth), **Social** mobile behaviour, and **Technological** AI adoption, constrained by **Legal** **DPDP** consent/retention and emerging **AI** accountability debates.  
• **Know Your Candidate** sits at **Legal** (**DPDP**, **Aadhaar** ecosystem), **Social** (fraud awareness), and **Technological** (verification APIs, AI screening), with **Political** undertones whenever **digital ID** touches **benefits** or **government** programmes.
