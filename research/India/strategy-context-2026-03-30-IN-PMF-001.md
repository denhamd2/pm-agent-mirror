## Strategic priorities (Q2 2026) relevant to India

**Priority 1: GCC market readiness (strategic)**  
- **Description**: WhatsApp/SMS, nationalisation reporting, Arabic localisation, GCC job boards via Broadbean.  
- **Target outcomes**: 10 GCC customer wins; zero product-related GCC deal blockers.  
- **India relevance**: **Indirect but instructive**. India buyers will compare omnichannel maturity (SMS, WhatsApp) and compliance narrative against the same bar set by regional suites; GCC investment proves Workday’s willingness to fund market-specific channels.  

**Priority 2: AI candidate matching (differentiation)**  
- **Description**: HiredScore activation, ranking/shortlisting, bias/explainability positioning.  
- **Target outcomes**: 5 beta tenants; ~20% time-to-fill improvement for beta customers.  
- **India relevance**: **High**. High-volume hiring and fraud pressure increase demand for defensible screening; AI must be framed with **DPDP** and emerging **India AI governance** expectations (human review, vendor/processor accountability), not as unchecked automation.  

**Priority 3: Core ATS parity (table stakes)**  
- **Description**: Bulk grid actions, mobile recruiter improvements, background check integrations, Paradox activation.  
- **Target outcomes**: Parity vs SAP/Oracle on key workflows; fewer “missing basic feature” objections.  
- **India relevance**: **High**. Bulk actions and mobile-first recruiter workflows align with volume hiring; BGV orchestration and partner ecosystem matter for **Know Your Candidate**.

**India strategic assessment (IN-PMF-001, new market entry lens)**  
- India is positioned as **growth scale**, not a greenfield “ignore until later” region: an **8-customer** Q2 wins target pairs with **DPDP** and **local job board** expectations.  
- For **new market entry** with **fraud / verification** and **high volume** themes, product strategy should stress: **trustworthy data handling**, **auditability of consent and retention**, **BGV/orchestration** and **dedupe** depth, and **realistic** positioning on **native** India channels and **Naukri-class** multipost (partner-led where Broadbean applies).  
- Competitive pressure from **India HR suites** and **value ATS** (omnichannel, INR packaging, BGV partner stories) is a positioning constraint; Workday’s answer remains **suite depth**, **enterprise security**, and **compliance-first** design **if** backed by India-relevant proof points and honest gap handling.  

## Competitive positioning (Q2 themes)

**Workday differentiation (from Q2 doc)**  
1. Suite depth (HCM + Recruiting + Talent + Learning)  
2. AI-powered (HiredScore + Paradox)  
3. Compliance-first (GDPR, EU AI Act, global privacy)  
4. Enterprise scale (Fortune 500 patterns, security model)  

**Competitive vulnerabilities (from Q2 doc)**  
- Job board coverage gaps in niche regions (Broadbean partnership boundary)  
- Mobile recruiter experience vs SAP  
- Interview scheduling where Paradox is not activated  

**India PMF overlay**: Vulnerabilities **intensify** where buyers expect **+91 SMS**, **WhatsApp**, and **in-product Naukri-class** posting; **Know Your Candidate** programmes often assume **Aadhaar-class** identity checks—position **BGV framework + partners** and **legal review** rather than implying native government verification unless delivered.  

## What is NOT a priority Q2 (from Q2 doc)

- Career site redesign (deferred Q3)  
- Talent pool AI recommendations (H2)  
- Video interview beyond HireVue (low demand)  
- Recruiting marketing automation (low ROI for enterprise)  

**Implication for India PMF**: Avoid packaging India entry recommendations as dependent on career site redesign; focus on **compliance**, **volume workflow**, **verification**, and **channel/board** pragmatics.
