## Strategic Priorities (Q2 2026)

**Priority 1: GCC Market Readiness (Strategic)**  
- **Description**: Close product gaps that block GCC sales; emphasise candidate messaging (WhatsApp, SMS), nationalisation reporting, Arabic RTL, and job boards via Broadbean.  
- **Target Outcomes**: 10 GCC customer wins by end Q2; zero product-related deal blockers in GCC pipeline.  
- **Regional relevance (India)**: **Indirect but important**—WhatsApp and enhanced candidate communication appear on the **near-term TA roadmap** (PDF), which aligns with India recruiter and candidate behaviour; **101** should still prioritise **India-specific** boards, DPDP, and local suite competitors ahead of copying GCC-only requirements.

**Priority 2: AI Candidate Matching (Differentiation)**  
- **Description**: Activate HiredScore for high-volume customers; ranking, shortlisting, predictive time-to-fill; bias detection and explainability (EU AI Act framing).  
- **Target Outcomes**: 5 beta tenants on AI matching by end Q2; ~20% time-to-fill reduction for beta customers.  
- **Regional relevance (India)**: **High**—India scale hiring and volume recruiter personas benefit directly; **120** RICE should reward recommendations that unblock activation, governance, and explainability in a **DPDP** context.

**Priority 3: Core ATS Parity (Table Stakes)**  
- **Description**: Bulk candidate actions, mobile recruiter improvements, background check integrations, Paradox activation to reduce scheduling friction.  
- **Target Outcomes**: Parity with SAP and Oracle on key workflows; fewer “missing basic feature” sales objections.  
- **Regional relevance (India)**: **High**—bulk, mobile, and scheduling are recurring India interview themes; parity work supports the **8-customer** scale goal.

## Competitive Positioning

**Workday differentiation (Q2 themes + PDF)**  
1. **Suite depth**: HCM + Recruiting + Talent + Learning versus point ATS or local bundles.  
2. **AI-powered**: HiredScore and Paradox embedded when activated—matching, scheduling, conversational experiences.  
3. **Compliance-first**: GDPR, EU AI Act, and **global privacy** narrative; for India, **DPDP** is the natural local anchor.  
4. **Enterprise scale**: Security model, global rollout patterns, Fortune 500 workflows.

**Competitive vulnerabilities (flag for 101 / 120)**  
- **Job board coverage**: Gaps in niche or must-post India boards—validate **Broadbean** before calling native build (per product strategy).  
- **Mobile**: SAP called out as strong on mobile recruiter experience; parity matters for India.  
- **Interview scheduling**: Requires **Paradox activation**—not default; India research often stresses scheduling load.

**Key messaging**  
- Trusted global suite with **local compliance** (DPDP, consent, retention) and **high-volume efficiency**.  
- AI and automation with **human oversight** and explainability—important for regulated and enterprise India accounts.
