## Strategic priorities (Q2 2026) relevant to India

**Priority 1 (global)**: GCC market readiness  
• **India relevance**: Indirect. GCC is the numbered Priority 1; India is a **separate regional row** with its own targets. Use GCC only as a benchmark for how Workday packages **omnichannel**, **compliance narrative**, and **local board** stories when selling in APAC.

**Priority 2 (global)**: AI candidate matching (HiredScore activation, bias and explainability; EU AI Act alignment in narrative)  
• **India relevance**: **High** for **high-volume hiring** and **fraud/KYC** adjacent use cases (prioritisation, throughput, consistent assessment). Position AI as **governed** (human review, audit, purpose limitation) alongside **DPDP** consent and retention design.

**Priority 3 (global)**: Core ATS parity (bulk actions, mobile recruiter, BGV vendor integrations, Paradox scheduling)  
• **India relevance**: **High**. Bulk grid, mobile experience, and **background check** integrations directly support **volume** and **Know Your Candidate** programmes.

## Regional expansion priorities (India)

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Strategic intent** | **Scale growth** with compliance and local distribution (boards) as named pillars; aligns with **new market entry** driver for IN-PMF-002 |
| **Compliance** | **DPDP Act 2023** and **DPDP Rules 2025** define a **phased** programme (notices, consent, retention, cross-border, Board adjudication). Product must support **configurable** privacy, **vendor accountability** for BGV, and **auditability**, not “certified compliance in a box” |
| **Competitive positioning** | Differentiate on **suite depth**, **enterprise security**, **hire-to-pay**, **UDMF**, and **native BGV business process framework**; be precise on **India-specific channel and board** gaps (see competitive matrix and Deployment Agent classifications) |
| **Themes for IN-PMF-002** | **High-volume hiring**: campus and GCC-style scale, automation, AI matching where licensed, mobile-first candidate and recruiter expectations. **Know Your Candidate**: explicit consent per check type, minimisation, retention/deletion, vendor governance, identity stack (incl. Aadhaar ecosystem constraints where applicable) |

## Competitive positioning (Q2 themes, applied to India)

**Workday differentiation** (from quarterly doc)  
1. **Suite depth**: HCM + Recruiting + Talent + Learning  
2. **AI-powered**: HiredScore + Paradox (activation and SKU clarity)  
3. **Compliance-first**: Global privacy leadership; **India**: DPDP-aligned **programme** design with Legal (060)  
4. **Enterprise scale**: Fortune 500 patterns, security model, global rollouts

**Competitive vulnerabilities** (global, with India inflection)  
• **Job board coverage**: Dependence on **Broadbean** and partner multiposters for **Naukri-class** boards; avoid promising **native direct** multipost without roadmap evidence  
• **Mobile**: Recruiter mobile parity still a sales risk vs incumbents who market **mobile-first** TA  
• **Scheduling**: Paradox **activation** required; not automatic parity vs point solutions

## What is NOT a priority Q2 (quarterly doc)

• Career site redesign (Q3)  
• Talent pool AI recommendations (H2)  
• Video interview integrations beyond HireVue  
• Recruiting marketing automation  

**Implication for IN-PMF-002**: Market-entry storytelling should **not** lean on career site redesign or broad marketing automation; anchor on **DPDP**, **volume workflows**, **BGV/KYC**, and **board strategy**.
