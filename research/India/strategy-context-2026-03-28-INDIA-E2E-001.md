## Strategic Priorities (Q2 2026)

### Priority 1: GCC Market Readiness (Strategic)

- **Description**: GCC expansion is the headline 2026 strategic priority; product gaps are treated as sales blockers. Work spans candidate communication (WhatsApp, SMS), nationalisation compliance (KSA Nitaqat, UAE Emiratisation, Kuwait reporting), Arabic RTL and country workflows, and job boards (Bayt.com, GulfTalent via Broadbean).
- **Target Outcomes**: 10 GCC customer wins by end Q2; zero product-related deal blockers in GCC pipeline.
- **India relevance**: **Indirect**. Sets the bar for how regional readiness (compliance, messaging, local boards) is executed; **101** should still benchmark India competitors against the same global parity narrative, but **RICE Business Impact** for purely GCC-only features stays low for an India-selected recommendation.

### Priority 2: AI Candidate Matching (Differentiation)

- **Description**: Activate HiredScore for high-volume customers; AI-powered ranking, shortlisting, predictive time-to-fill; bias detection and explainability framed for EU AI Act alignment.
- **Target Outcomes**: 5 beta tenants on AI matching by end Q2; ~20% time-to-fill reduction for beta customers.
- **India relevance**: **Direct**. India scale hiring and IT/services volume customers align with AI matching ROI narratives; **120** should treat AI-heavy recommendations as **strong strategic alignment** when evidence supports adoption and responsible use (map to India DPDP and consent in downstream PESTEL/Legal, not assumed here).

### Priority 3: Core ATS Parity (Table Stakes)

- **Description**: Bulk candidate actions, mobile recruiter experience, background check integrations (HireRight, Checkr), interview scheduling friction reduction via Paradox activation.
- **Target Outcomes**: Parity with SAP/Oracle on key workflows; no "missing basic feature" sales objections.
- **India relevance**: **Direct**. India growth depends on table-stakes wins in high-volume workflows; **101** should prioritise gaps that show up in enterprise RFPs and recruiter efficiency (bulk, mobile, scheduling).

## Competitive Positioning

**Workday differentiation** (Q2 themes):

1. **Suite depth**: HCM + Recruiting + Talent + Learning versus point ATS vendors.
2. **AI-powered**: HiredScore + Paradox when activated versus manual workflows.
3. **Compliance-first**: GDPR, EU AI Act, global privacy positioning versus reactive competitors.
4. **Enterprise scale**: Fortune 500 workflows, security model, global rollouts.

**Competitive vulnerabilities** (watch for India-specific evidence in **101** / **105**):

- Job board coverage gaps (Broadbean partnership limits in niche or local channels).
- Mobile recruiter experience versus SAP.
- Interview scheduling parity when Paradox is not activated.

**Key messaging** (GTM / deck hooks for **130** Product Strategy slides):

- Land **trust and compliance** (DPDP, consent, auditability) alongside **volume efficiency** (AI matching, bulk actions, scheduling).
- Avoid promising **native** job board builds where **Broadbean** expansion is the stated strategy (see **010-style-guide** / product context).
