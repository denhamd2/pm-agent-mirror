# PESTEL — India Recruiting (INDIA-E2E-006)

**Date:** 7 May 2026 | **Mission:** INDIA-E2E-006

| Factor | Assessment (India recruiting context) |
|--------|----------------------------------------|
| **Political** | Central labour-code narratives and **state-level** employment rules keep compliance documentation salient; **digital public infrastructure** (Aadhaar ecosystem) influences identity workflows but is **politically sensitive** for mandatory product positioning. |
| **Economic** | **High-volume hiring** segments (IT services, GCC/BPO, retail frontline) optimise for **cost-per-hire** and **time-to-fill**; tooling must reduce **manual reconciliation** (email approvals, spreadsheet SLAs) that shows up in customer transcripts. |
| **Social** | **WhatsApp** as default social graph for candidates; expectation of **quick two-way replies**; **trust deficit** on unknown numbers → branded, consent-based messaging matters. **Email** still carries **formality** for documents and offer letters. |
| **Technological** | **eKYC / OTP** delivery variance; **deepfake** and **document fraud** risk in remote hiring; API-first verification vendors; need for **threading** and **message-level audit** in core UI. |
| **Environmental** | Low direct recruiting impact; indirect **paper-offer** reduction supports digital-first hiring in urban hubs. |
| **Legal** | **DPDP** consent/notice/retention; **IT Act** and **spam** norms for commercial messaging; **cross-border** processing when global employers hire in India; **EU AI Act** overlay for EU deployers on **AI-assisted matching** (triangulate with 060 in PRD). |

**Synthesis:** Legal–social–technical cluster is **tightest**: omnichannel comms and KYC must be designed as **one trust surface**, not three disconnected features.
