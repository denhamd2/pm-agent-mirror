# Workday Recruiting Product Priorities Q2 2026

## Executive Summary

Q2 2026 focuses on **global expansion** (GCC, APAC) and **AI-powered efficiency** while maintaining competitive parity in core ATS capabilities. GM Adam Godson's April 2026 all-hands reinforced a unified TA pillar revenue target of **$176M** (~18% growth on FY26's $987M) and a three-part strategy: a connected TA suite, best-of-class agents, and automating admin to increase human connection.

## GM Strategic Context (April 2026 All-Hands, Adam Godson)

### Revenue and Financial Position
- **FY26 actual**: $987M TA pillar revenue (close to $1B milestone - not yet celebrated)
- **FY27 target**: $176M unified TA pillar revenue (~18% growth)
- Revenue goal covers all SKUs as one: Workday Recruiting, HiredScore, Paradox, Candidate Engagement - "one team, one revenue goal"
- Workday under economic and stock price pressure; growth must be proven quarter after quarter to justify valuation

### Three-Part Strategy
1. **TA Suite that works together**: Workday Recruiting + its agents as a connected system fulfilling the HCM value proposition. Opening the platform with more APIs and integration paths. Connected platform is the external message.
2. **Best-of-class features through agents**: Acquired agents (Paradox, HiredScore), first-party agents (Requisition Agent, Interview Teams Agent), citizen-built agents (via Sana Agent Builder), and third-party agents where strategic.
3. **Automate admin, increase human connection**: The goal is NOT to automate everything in TA - automate administrative work so humans can connect. Design should reflect this principle.

### Five Goal Areas for FY27
1. **Revenue** ($176M) - the scoreboard and basis for freedom to operate
2. **Easy to understand and buy** - simple messaging, clear value, remove sales blockers
3. **Build stuff** - product delivery including compliance and security (not just "sexy" features)
4. **Easy to deploy and serve** - reduce HiredScore/Paradox deployment backlog, enable client self-serve
5. **Future forward** - next-gen agents, AI coding for career sites (Ho Chi Minh City team under Liz Ribello), Universal Profile initiative, Limor Peleg matching improvements in HiredScore, job distribution on AI platforms

### Competitive Landscape Shift (GM View)
- **New competitors**: Microsoft, ServiceNow - not just SAP, Oracle, Dayforce, Cornerstone. The question is now "how does work get done?" broadly, including agents.
- **In-house build threat**: Customers' internal teams building their own solutions using AI coding is a major new competitive threat.
- **AI in TA is mainstream**: No longer novel; no longer enough to say "we do AI matching". Must articulate differentiated value (compliant, in-flow-of-work, bias-tested).
- **AI coding impact**: Prototyping easier than ever for everyone. Back-to-basics moment for product management - deeply understand core value, interconnectedness, network effects. Features race is over; business strategy matters more.

### Why We Win (GM Framing)
- **Customer trust**: Always in buying cycles, trusted brand
- **Connection to core system**: Real-time business data, HCM integration advantage not to be understated
- **Domain expertise**: Decade+ of recruiting expertise (compliance, operations depth). Amateurs entering the space miss a lot.
- **Innovative spirit + strong PM**: Discerning choices about bleeding edge vs. what customers will buy today. Paint the vision, then earn the right to get customers to step 10 by starting at step 3.
- **Best team**: Global, 15+ time zones, diverse cultures and perspectives

### Opportunity Areas Called Out by GM
- **Reporting reimagined**: Weekly funnel pings via Teams/Slack (activity, probability, pipeline) - not traditional dashboards
- **Interviewing and selection**: Structured interview support, better debrief, interview feedback summarisation (already shipped). Unstructured interviews have R-squared of ~0.2 - we can help.
- **Career sites**: Agent-to-agent communication, content distributed on AI platforms (ChatGPT, Claude, Google). Consumer behaviour is changing.
- **API-first development**: Max's leadership direction - foundational work to prepare for AI-driven development future. Offsite outcomes expected soon.

### Candidate Engagement / CRM Strategy
- "Somewhere in the middle" priority - not top, not unimportant
- Open question: will CRM as a category persist in its current form for the next 10 years?
- Events features available as land motion to non-Workday ATS customers
- Paradox iframe integration being replaced by extension, then Workday UI as only path (per Muli Farkas)

### AI Fluency
- Individual-level AI fluency for all team members is an operational excellence goal
- Enabled through Reforge (product), training activities, and tool access
- Adam encouraging teams to prototype and swing at innovation ideas using AI coding

## Recruiting Product Priorities

### Priority 1: GCC Market Readiness (Strategic)
**Why**: GCC expansion is 2026 strategic priority; product gaps block sales.

**Key Initiatives**:
- **Candidate communication**: WhatsApp and SMS integration (GCC cultural fit, 98% open rates)
- **Nationalisation compliance**: KSA Nitaqat, UAE Emiratisation, Kuwait labor law reporting
- **Localisation**: Arabic RTL support, country-specific workflows
- **Job boards**: Bayt.com, GulfTalent via Broadbean partnership

**Success Metrics**: 
- 10 GCC customer wins by end Q2
- Zero product-related deal blockers in GCC pipeline

**Competitive Context**: SAP SuccessFactors and Oracle have GCC-specific features; we're catching up.

### Priority 2: AI Candidate Matching (Differentiation)
**Why**: HiredScore acquisition needs to show ROI; AI is competitive differentiator.

**Key Initiatives**:
- Activate HiredScore for high-volume customers
- AI-powered candidate ranking and shortlisting
- Predictive analytics for time-to-fill
- Bias detection and explainability (EU AI Act compliance)

**Success Metrics**:
- 5 beta tenants using AI matching by end Q2
- 20% reduction in time-to-fill for beta customers

**Competitive Context**: Paradox (scheduling) and HiredScore (matching) give us AI edge over Oracle/SAP.

### Priority 3: Core ATS Parity (Table Stakes)
**Why**: Can't win if we lack basic bulk actions, mobile, or integrations.

**Key Initiatives**:
- Bulk candidate actions (reject, advance, email)
- Mobile recruiter experience improvements
- Background check vendor integrations (HireRight, Checkr)
- Interview scheduling friction reduction (Paradox activation)

**Success Metrics**:
- Feature parity with SAP/Oracle on key workflows
- Zero "missing basic feature" objections in sales cycles

## Competitive Positioning (Q2 Themes)

### Workday Differentiation
1. **Suite depth**: HCM + Recruiting + Talent + Learning (vs. point solutions)
2. **AI-powered**: HiredScore + Paradox (vs. manual workflows) - but must articulate differentiated value beyond "we do AI" (compliance, in-flow-of-work, bias-tested)
3. **Compliance-first**: GDPR, EU AI Act, global data privacy (vs. reactive competitors)
4. **Enterprise scale**: Fortune 500 workflows, security model, global rollouts
5. **Connected system advantage**: Real-time HCM data, network effects, interconnectedness of tools - this is the core natural advantage per GM
6. **Domain expertise**: Decade+ recruiting expertise that new entrants lack (compliance, operations, structured selection)

### Competitive Vulnerabilities
- **Job board coverage**: Broadbean partnership gaps in niche regions
- **Mobile**: SAP has better mobile recruiter app (we're catching up)
- **Interview scheduling**: Paradox activation required (not default)
- **Deployment backlog**: Long HiredScore and Paradox deployment queues limiting time-to-value
- **New entrants**: Microsoft and ServiceNow thinking about recruiting/work space; in-house teams building with AI coding tools
- **Messaging clarity**: AI in TA is mainstream; "we have AI" is no longer differentiating - need sharper value articulation

## What's NOT a Priority Q2

To maintain focus, these are explicitly **not** Q2 priorities:
- Career site redesign (deferred to Q3 - though early R&D on agent-to-agent and AI platform distribution is "future forward")
- Talent pool AI recommendations (deferred to H2)
- Video interview integrations beyond HireVue (low demand)
- Recruiting marketing automation (low ROI for enterprise)
- CRM / Candidate Engagement major investment ("somewhere in the middle" per GM - strategy still being evaluated; events features continue as land motion)

## RICE Guidance for Q2

When scoring recommendations this quarter:

**Business Impact scoring** (aligned with GM's 5 goal areas):
- 3.0: GCC market readiness, AI matching, deployment backlog reduction (strategic priorities + "easy to deploy")
- 2.0: Core ATS parity gaps blocking sales, messaging simplification ("easy to understand and buy")
- 1.0: Quality-of-life improvements for existing customers, operational excellence, AI fluency
- 0.5: Nice-to-have features, edge cases, future-forward R&D (unless specifically called out by GM)
- 0.25: Not aligned with Q2 focus or GM's goal structure

**Example**:
- WhatsApp for GCC: Business Impact 3.0 (strategic priority) + Customer Impact 2.0 (high pain) = Composite 2.5
- Career site redesign: Business Impact 0.5 (not Q2 priority) + Customer Impact 1.5 (medium pain) = Composite 1.0
