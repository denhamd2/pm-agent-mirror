# JTBD excerpt: HR Professional (Recruiter) and Manager (hiring manager)

Curated from the internal **JTBD Worksheet** for use with **315-ux-designer** and **320-prototype-developer**. This file is the **stable agent-facing source**; update when the canonical worksheet taxonomy changes.

**Persona names in the worksheet**: **HR Professional** (Talent Acquisition and related) ≈ **Recruiter** in Recruiting prototypes; **Manager** ≈ **hiring manager** when the feature supports **Grow and maintain my team** hiring jobs.

---

## HR Professional (Recruiter) – Talent Acquisition cluster

### People strategy enablement (context for recruiter-facing strategy work)

High-level themes (not exhaustive): partner with people leaders; understand business needs and strategy; keep leaders informed; validate business needs; create and implement people/talent strategies; support HR programmes; workforce insights; data quality and security; coordinate workforce planning across TA, Finance, and the Business.

### Talent Acquisition – jobs to be done

**Attract the right talent**

- Publish job postings that attract the right candidates  
- Source high quality candidates for active job requisitions  
- Maintain a pool of prospective candidates for future sourcing needs  

**Manage candidates throughout the recruiting process**

- Determine if candidate meets requirements of the job  
- Progress candidates through the stages of the pipeline as efficiently as possible  
- Make a successful offer to the chosen candidate  
- *(Implicit in worksheet structure: keep candidates engaged through the process)*  

**Manage my assigned job requisitions**

- Manage my assigned job requisitions  
- Keep track of JRs across pipeline stages  
- Understand which job reqs are most in need of attention  
- Ensure that job requisitions have healthy statuses  
- Ensure that the details of each job requisition is accurate and up to date  
- Understand where in the approvals process a request or job requisition currently sits  

**Be an effective partner to hiring teams**

- Collaborate with hiring teams to hire the right candidate quickly  
- Help hiring teams understand the progress and status of a job requisition  
- Anticipate the hiring needs of teams ahead of time  
- Keep track of my team's status and challenges  

**Foster an inclusive and equitable recruiting process**

- Ensure that there is a diverse candidate slate prior to hiring for an open job requisition  
- Understand the levels of candidate diversity within open job requisitions  
- Ensure that candidates are assessed in a fair, equitable manner  
- Identify areas of improvement in the recruiting process for better diversity outcomes  

**Maintain high standards of efficiency and effectiveness**

- Meet my efficiency metrics as a recruiter  
- Maintain data integrity throughout the recruiting process  
- Keep track of concurrent tasks that need to be completed  
- Ensure that time is made to action high priority items  
- Understand my capacity to take on extra projects  
- Identify ways that my HR systems can better meet my workflow

## Manager (hiring manager) – recruiting-relevant jobs

For Recruiting prototypes, prioritise **Grow and maintain my team** jobs that touch hiring. Other manager outcomes (time off, performance, culture, etc.) apply when the PRD explicitly spans them.

### Grow and maintain my team (hiring subset)

- Identify a gap on my team  
- Coordinate hiring needs with others  
- Determine if a candidate is the right fit  
- Track the progress of candidates through the hiring process  
- Finalize candidate's offer  
- Onboard employee onto the team  
- Provide relevant materials [during onboarding]  
- Provide relevant support [during onboarding]  
- Integrate employee into team rituals/processes  
- Create plan for new employee  

*(Related: off-boarding, team success, and culture jobs apply when the feature touches handoffs or visibility.)*

---

## Worker (candidate) – when the PRD names the candidate as primary actor

Use the worksheet’s **Worker** outcomes only when discovery is candidate-centric (e.g. career site, application, offer acceptance). Examples of useful clusters:

- **Choose the right job for me**: prepare for job search; find a relevant job; assess fit; apply; track application; engage with hiring organisation; accept or decline offer  
- **Set myself up for success at work** / **Do my job effectively**: as relevant to pre-hire or first-day experiences in scope

## How to use in discovery (for 315)

1. Read **`docs/jtbd-recruiting-hr-professional-and-manager.md`** after the PRD.  
2. Pick **primary** persona (usually Recruiter; sometimes Manager; sometimes Worker).  
3. Copy **1 high-level outcome** and **1–3 verbatim JTBD lines** from the tables above into the Discovery Brief.  
4. Write one **synthesised** sentence: *When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [outcome]*.  
5. List **prototype implications** (what the UI must prove for that job).
