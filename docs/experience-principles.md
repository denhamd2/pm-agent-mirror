# Workday Experience Principles

**How Workday's strategy applies to the human experience of using our software.**

These principles guide every design decision toward elevating humans, supercharging work, and moving business forever forward.

---

## The Three Principles

### 1. Empower
**Give Users Control**
> Stay out of the way, amplify the human.

Humans stay in control while the tool provides a balance of flexibility and power.

#### Outcome-Focused
- ✅ **We must** focus on achieving the user's desired outcome.
- ❌ **We don't** force users to work through the system's functional model.

#### Augment over Replace
- ✅ **We must** keep the human in control as an active driver of the process.
- ❌ **We don't** obscure information, creating a sense of helplessness.

#### Stay Out of the Way
- ✅ **We must** let the tool recede into the background.
- ❌ **We don't** require users to "earn a degree" in order to adopt basic functions.

---

### 2. Trust
**Build Their Confidence**
> No surprises, only confidence.

All users should feel informed, capable, and enthusiastic when they use Workday software - clear previews, transparency, easy iteration, and feedback that builds reliability.

#### Transparency
- ✅ **We ensure** users understand what the system is doing.
- ❌ **We don't** leave users guessing at how or why the system acts.

#### Clarity & Familiarity
- ✅ **We create** a product that speaks the user's language and feels like a natural part of their process.
- ❌ **We don't** use unusual terms that obscure meaning or create confusion.

#### Quality with Accuracy
- ✅ **We build** products with a quality that creates a sense of reliability.
- ❌ **We don't** build slop that sows doubt and hesitation.

---

### 3. Grow
**Enable Them To Change**
> Always evolving, never rigid.

The system grows with the user and the business – flexible, resilient, with low friction for change. Revisions are easy and updates are smooth.

#### Easy Change
- ✅ **We enable** easy revision and iterative improvement through self-service.
- ❌ **We don't** punish change, forcing dependence on technical support or complex coordination across orgs.

#### Clarity & Familiarity
- ✅ **We provide** clear histories of changes and their implications.
- ❌ **We don't** require users to become detectives to piece together a view of how the system has changed.

#### Quality with Accuracy
- ✅ **We make it easy** to build upon existing work and integrate across systems.
- ❌ **We don't** require users to manually re-implement their content in our newest solutions.

## About the Principles

### What are principles?
Principles are core themes that represent our strategic point of view on what makes good design for our products. They serve as guideposts for creating the most valuable design discussions and ultimately support strategic decision making.

### Where & when do I apply principles?

These principles become powerful once integrated into our daily design and development processes:

- **Strategic Alignment**: Ensure design decisions align with broader business strategy and user needs.
- **Cross-Disciplinary Collaboration**: Use the principles to identify and decide on product tradeoffs with engineering, product management, and business stakeholders.
- **AI-Enhanced PRDs**: Include principles in AI prompts to help write more strategic Product Requirements Documents.
- **AI Prototyping**: Use principles to guide AI-powered prototyping tools for faster iteration cycles.

### What makes good principles?

Effective design principles share certain key characteristics that make them valuable for strategic decision making:

- **Clear & Actionable**: Easy to understand, providing specific guidance for design decisions.
- **Strategically Aligned**: Connecting product interactions to broader business strategy.
- **Uniquely Ours**: Extend beyond generic design best practices.

### How are principles different from heuristics or tenets?

- **Principles**: Strategic POV of what is good in our product design. Help with strategic decision making.
- **Heuristics**: Universal rules-of-thumb or best-practices that apply to nearly any software design. Can be generic or domain specific.
- **Tenets**: Core beliefs for how we work together to achieve operational excellence in a collaborative environment.

### How were the principles created?

Built from our company vision and strategic foundation, the principles guide every design decision toward elevating humans, supercharging work, and moving business forever forward.

**Our Company Vision:**
> "We're moving businesses forever forward, elevating humans and supercharging work by giving organizations the power to transform how they manage their people and their money."

- **Elevating Humans**: Humans are your greatest asset.
- **Supercharging Work**: Unlock unparalleled productivity.
- **Moving Business Forward**: Never standing still, always in motion.

## Application in Product Development

### For Product Managers (PRDs)

When writing PRDs, validate features against all three principles:

1. **Empower**: Does this feature keep users in control? Is it outcome-focused?
2. **Trust**: Will users understand what's happening? Does it use familiar language?
3. **Grow**: Can users easily revise/iterate? Are changes smooth?

### For UX Designers (Discovery & Design)

When creating Discovery Briefs and design specifications:

1. **Empower**: Design for the user's outcome, not the system's model
2. **Trust**: Ensure transparency, clarity, and reliability in every interaction
3. **Grow**: Make change easy, provide history, enable building upon existing work

### For UX Writers (Copy & Messaging)

When writing UI copy and messaging:

1. **Empower**: Use language that reinforces user control
2. **Trust**: Write clearly, avoid jargon, build confidence
3. **Grow**: Explain changes, provide context, use familiar terms

### For Prototype Developers (Implementation)

When building prototypes:

1. **Empower**: Implement controls that give users agency
2. **Trust**: Build with quality, show clear states, provide feedback
3. **Grow**: Design for iteration, make changes visible, enable self-service

## Quick Reference: Principle Application

| Principle | Key Question | Red Flag |
|-----------|--------------|----------|
| **Empower** | "Does this keep the user in control?" | User forced through system's model, not their goal |
| **Trust** | "Will the user understand what's happening?" | Unclear states, unfamiliar terms, low quality |
| **Grow** | "Can the user easily change this later?" | Change is punishing, requires support, breaks existing work |

## Examples in Recruiting Context

### Empower (Recruiter Control)
- ✅ **Good**: Recruiter can bulk-update req status with preview before applying
- ❌ **Bad**: System auto-moves candidates through pipeline without recruiter approval

### Trust (Transparency & Clarity)
- ✅ **Good**: "3 candidates will be notified via email. Preview →"
- ❌ **Bad**: "Processing..." (no indication of what's happening or to whom)

### Grow (Easy Change)
- ✅ **Good**: Edit campaign message after sending (affects future sends, history preserved)
- ❌ **Bad**: Must recreate entire campaign to fix one typo in message template
