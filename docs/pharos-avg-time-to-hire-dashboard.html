Overall Monthly Trend

Average days to hire across all active tenants (non-zero values only)

Insight: Time to hire has been relatively stable at 68-77 days over the past 14 months, with seasonal upticks in December-January (holiday slowdowns) and a slight downward trend through mid-2025 before rebounding.
By Geographic Region

Segmented by data centre location (ATL=Americas-East, PDX/ORE=Americas-West, DUB=EMEA, MTL=Canada, SIN=APAC)

Insight: EMEA consistently has the highest time to hire (73-88 days), peaking at 87.7 days in Jan 2026. Canada is the fastest major region (~55-61 days). APAC data is sparse (drops off after May 2025) but trended lower when available. Americas-East and West track closely at 65-80 days.
Tenant Distribution

How tenants cluster by average time to hire (last 90 days, distinct tenants per bucket)

Insight: The largest active cohort is 31-60 days (2,380 tenants), followed by 61-90 days (1,817). A significant 3,154 tenants report zero (no completed hires in reporting period). 569 tenants exceed 120 days - potential candidates for intervention or data quality review.
By Cloud Infrastructure

Monthly trend segmented by hosting type (AWS, GCP, Workday DC)

Insight: AWS-hosted tenants consistently hire ~15-20 days faster than Workday DC tenants (53-61 vs 70-81 days). This likely reflects that AWS tenants tend to be newer, cloud-native customers with more modern hiring processes rather than an infrastructure performance difference. GCP tenants sit in between (64-77 days). AWS tenant count is growing rapidly (797 to 1,278).

Adoption Growth

Monthly count of tenants with non-zero Average Time to Hire (active recruiting)

Insight: Active recruiting tenants grew from 3,012 (Jan 2025) to 3,403 (Feb 2026) - a 13% increase in adoption. The December dip to 2,536 reflects holiday-period inactivity. The Feb 2026 figure of 3,403 is the highest on record, suggesting accelerating platform adoption.
